# Module 4 — Winemaking Technology

**Vineyard Business Plan — Huși, Vaslui County, Romania**
Last updated: May 2026

## 4.1 Overview

This module covers the physical infrastructure and equipment needed to convert the existing agricultural shed into a certified winery and produce DOC-quality Muscat Ottonel. The operation targets:

- **Scale:** 50–80 tonnes of grapes per year → 300–500 hl wine → 35,000–50,000 bottles
- **Quality tier:** DOC Dealurile Huși (CMD and CT), aromatic dry and off-dry white, with indigenous variety complementary tiers
- **Budget envelope:** €50k Year 1 / €200k total over 3 years
- **Key constraint:** Muscat Ottonel's terpene-driven character (linalool, geraniol, nerol) is volatile and easily damaged by heat, oxygen, and harsh fining — every equipment and process decision must protect aromatic expression

## 4.2 Winery Layout & Shed Conversion

### Space Requirements

A 50–80 tonne operation requires **300–500 m²** of total floor space. The functional zones and their minimum areas:

| Zone | Area (m²) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Reception / crush pad | 40–60 | Covered outdoor canopy preferred; forklift access essential |
| Press room | 25–40 | Press body + juice trough + hose routing to tanks |
| Fermentation room | 80–150 | Dominant zone; glycol-jacketed tanks require wide aisle clearance |
| Aging / storage cellar | 60–100 | Tank storage; barrel program if any |
| Bottling area | 20–40 | Can be shared with storage when not in use |
| Laboratory | 8–15 | Bench + water + ventilation; fume extraction for volatile solvents |
| Cleaning / utility room | 10–20 | Chemical storage, CIP trolley, compressed air line |
| Cold stabilisation | 0–30 | Integrated into fermentation room if tanks have glycol jackets reaching −5°C |

**Practical note:** Industry experience says the theoretical minimum should be doubled for real-world operations (forklift movement, aisles, consultants, consumables storage). Target 350–400 m² if the shed allows.

### Ceiling Height and Flow Design

- **Minimum ceiling height:** 4.5–5 m for forklift operation with half-ton fruit bins
- **Pump-based (flat floor):** The practical choice for an agricultural shed conversion in Huși; gravity-flow requires a hillside site or expensive split-level construction
- **Standard 5–6 m industrial shed height** is workable with pump-based design

### Floor Specification

- **Slope:** 1–2% toward trench drains in press and tank zones
- **Drainage:** Stainless-steel (ACO-type) trench drains; 5–10 L/min per metre capacity for harvest wash-down volumes
- **Floor coating:** **Polyurethane mortar (urethane cement), 6–9 mm** — the food-safe standard for wet winery areas. Standard epoxy coatings are not suitable (delaminate under hot CIP water above 60°C). Cost in Romania: €40–80/m² supply and apply.
- **Load bearing:** 15 cm C20/25 concrete slab adequate for tanks up to 5,000 L; tanks 10,000 L+ require structural engineer assessment

### Temperature Requirements by Zone

| Zone | Target Temp | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| White wine fermentation | 14–16°C | **Critical for Muscat Ottonel** — terpenes (linalool, geraniol) volatilise rapidly above 18–20°C |
| Post-fermentation settling | 4–8°C | Cold static clarification (débourbage); 12–24 hours |
| Cold stabilisation | −4 to −1°C | 10–21 days unseeded; 24–48 hours with potassium bitartrate seeding |
| Storage tanks | 12–15°C | Prevents refermentation and oxidation during bulk storage |
| Bottle storage | 12–15°C | Standard cellar range |

### Water Requirements

- **Benchmark:** 2.1–2.2 L water per litre of wine produced (winery operations only; not vineyard irrigation)
- **For 400 hl/year:** ~150,000–200,000 L/year (150–200 m³), spiking heavily during the 2–4 week harvest period
- **Well adequacy:** A well yielding 3–5 m³/hour provides comfortable headroom; supplement with a 5,000–10,000 L holding cistern in the shed for peak demand
- **Potability:** ANSVSA requires tested and certified potable water; a treatment system may be needed if the well shows any microbiological indicators

### Regulatory Registration

**ANSVSA (Vaslui County DSVSA):**
1. Register the premises as a food production facility before any commercial production
2. Obtain *autorizație sanitară veterinară* after physical inspection
3. Implement a simplified HACCP plan; inspector verifies drainage, washable surfaces, chemical segregation, pest control, and staff hygiene facilities
4. Submit potability certificate for the well water

**ONVPV (RPV Online portal — onvpv.ro):**
1. Register vineyards in the Vineyard Plantations Register (*Registrul Plantațiilor Viticole*)
2. Register as grape and wine producer via the SNIIV portal
3. Submit annual harvest, production, and stock declarations
4. Each vintage requires DOC certification sampling and sensory panel approval by an ONVPV-accredited lab

**EU labelling (Regulation (EU) 2021/2117):** All still wines must carry an ingredients list and nutritional information from December 2023; this can be delivered via an e-label QR code on the back label.

## 4.3 Reception & Pressing Equipment

### Pneumatic Press

The pneumatic press is the most important single piece of equipment for an aromatic white wine operation. A rubber bladder inflates against the fruit inside a perforated cylindrical cage, pressing juice out gently in programmable pressure stages (0.2–2.0 bar). Successive fractions allow separation of free-run juice (low phenolics, highest aroma) from pressed fractions.

**Why essential for Muscat Ottonel:**
- Progressive gentle squeeze minimises skin/seed maceration and phenolic extraction
- Closed-cage presses with inert gas capability (N₂ or CO₂) protect terpene precursors from oxidation during pressing — the single most impactful intervention for preserving linalool and geraniol in the juice
- Demonstrably superior aromatic outcomes vs. basket presses or open-bladder presses

**Sizing:** A **20–25 hl press** handles 10 ha at peak harvest (8–12 tonnes/day = 3–4 press loads/day at 3-hour cycles).

**Indicative pricing:**

| Bucher Vaslin Inertys (inert gas) | 40 hl | €35,000–55,000 | Premium inert-gas option |
| Della Toffola / Pera Smart Press | 20 hl | €15,000–25,000 | Competitive pricing |

**Year 1 strategy:** Use a cooperative's press (Domeniile Averești, a Huși cooperative, or Cotnari) in the first vintage. This frees capital for tanks and chiller — the higher-priority items for quality control. Own a press by Year 2.

### Crusher-Destemmer

Optional for whole-cluster pressing but recommended: even a light crush dramatically speeds press cycles and improves juice yield by 5–10% on Muscat Ottonel's large, juicy berries.

- Entry-level roller-type units (Grifo, Scharfenberger, AEB): **€1,500–4,000 new**, €500–1,500 used
- Run at minimum gap settings to destem without crushing seed if preferred

### Grape Reception and Sorting

- **Reception hopper + screw conveyor** (0.5–1.5 tonne capacity, 3–4 m lift): €2,000–5,000 new; Year 1 alternative is forklift dumping of half-ton bins
- **Vibrating sorting table** (perforated conveyor, workers on each side): €3,000–8,000 new; adds genuine value for DOC production from 10 ha, especially for late-season botrytis selection in September

### Grape/Must Pump

| Pump Type | Shear | Recommended Use |
|---|---|---|
| Centrifugal | High — damages berries | Not recommended for must |
| Eccentric screw (progressive cavity) | Very low | **Best all-purpose** — handles whole berries, must, finished wine |
| Peristaltic (hose pump) | Extremely low | Ideal for wine-to-bottling transfers |

**Recommendation:** An **eccentric screw pump** (Ragazzini Rotho or SRAML equivalent, 95 L/min) as the main workhorse. Cost new: €2,500–6,000; used: €800–2,500. Add a peristaltic pump in Year 3 for final wine transfers to the bottling tank.

## 4.4 Fermentation Equipment

### Stainless Steel Tanks

**Variable-capacity (floating lid) tanks with glycol cooling jackets** are the definitive choice for Muscat Ottonel. The floating lid sits on the wine surface on an inflatable gasket, eliminating headspace — critical for an oxidation-sensitive aromatic white.

**Essential tank features:**
- Glycol or dimple cooling jacket (lower half or full-side) for temperature control
- Variable-capacity floating lid with inflatable gasket
- Cone or dish bottom for lees settling and clean racking
- Sample valve at mid-height; pressure-vacuum relief valve; CIP ball/spray inlet
- Food-grade 304 stainless steel (316L for glycol-contact surfaces)

**Suggested tank configuration for 300–500 hl:**

| Tank Size | Quantity | Total HL | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| 50 hl (5,000 L) | 4 | 200 hl | Main fermentation / bulk storage |
| 30 hl (3,000 L) | 3 | 90 hl | Press fractions, special lots |
| 20 hl (2,000 L) | 2 | 40 hl | Reserve / late harvest selections |
| 10 hl (1,000 L) | 2 | 20 hl | Trial batches, blending tests |
| **Total** | **11 tanks** | **~350 hl working** | 70% utilisation factor |

**Indicative new pricing (with glycol jacket + variable lid):**

| Capacity | Price Range (€) | Price/hl |
|---|---|---|
| 1,000 L (10 hl) | €1,500–3,000 | €150–300/hl |
| 2,000 L (20 hl) | €2,500–4,500 | €125–225/hl |
| 3,000 L (30 hl) | €3,500–6,000 | €117–200/hl |
| 5,000 L (50 hl) | €5,000–9,000 | €100–180/hl |

Used equipment costs 30–50% of new. **Key suppliers serving Romania/Eastern Europe:** Letina (Croatia), Marchisio (Italy), Speidel (Germany), Polsinelli Enologia (Italy).

**Concrete eggs and oak barrels:** Not recommended as primary vessels for Muscat Ottonel. Concrete's micro-oxygenation dulls terpene freshness; oak masks varietal aromatics. A small program of 4–6 used neutral 500 L tonneaux (€800–2,700 used) in Year 3 is appropriate for experimenting with Zghihară de Huși and reserve blends only.

### Glycol Chiller

A glycol chiller (propylene glycol + water circuit) circulates chilled fluid through tank jackets. It drives both fermentation temperature control and cold stabilisation.

**Sizing:** Peak demand occurs during harvest when 5–6 tanks (200–250 hl) ferment simultaneously:
- Active fermentation generates ~640 BTU/hr per hl
- For 250 hl simultaneous fermentation: ~160,000 BTU/hr = **13–14 tons refrigeration** at peak
- Cold stabilisation requires 5–6 tons sustained

**Practical target: 10–15 ton (35–53 kW) glycol chiller** for this scale.

| Chiller Size | New Price (€) | Used Price (€) |
|---|---|---|
| 5-ton (Year 1 bridging) | €8,000–15,000 | €4,000–8,000 |
| 10-ton | €18,000–30,000 | €6,000–12,000 |
| 15-ton | €28,000–45,000 | €10,000–18,000 |

Budget an additional **€3,000–8,000** for insulated glycol distribution piping, solenoid valves, and per-tank temperature controllers.

**Year 1 bridge option:** An ice water bath (insulated vessel + small circulation pump) can cool 1–2 tanks at a time for a minimal first harvest. Labour-intensive; not suitable beyond Year 1.

## 4.5 Finishing & Bottling

### Filtration

**Recommended sequence for Muscat Ottonel dry white:**

1. **Gross clarification:** Cold static settling (débourbage) at 4–8°C for 12–24 hours; rack off gross lees — no equipment cost beyond a cold jacketed tank
2. **Polishing pre-bottling:** Plate-and-frame pad filter (20-plate Padovan or equivalent) at rough/medium grade
3. **Sterile fill:** 0.45 µm absolute membrane cartridge filter inline before the bottling valve

**Why not cross-flow?** Cross-flow (tangential-flow) membrane filtration is economically justified above ~1,000 hl/year; at €20,000–40,000 new, it is not warranted at this scale.

**Investment:** Plate-and-frame filter (used) + sterile cartridge housing: **€2,000–5,000** total. Sterile cartridges cost €50–150 each and handle 20–50 hl per run.

### Cold Stabilisation

Potassium bitartrate crystals must be removed before bottling to avoid precipitate complaints from buyers. Procedure:
1. Cool wine to −2 to −4°C (just above wine's freezing point: approximately −(ABV/2)°C)
2. Optional: seed with 2–4 g/L powdered potassium bitartrate to accelerate crystal nucleation
3. Hold 10–21 days (unseeded) or 24–48 hours (seeded)
4. Rack while still cold

No additional tank is needed if existing glycol-jacketed tanks can reach −5°C. A dedicated cold room (15–20 m², insulated panels) costs €5,000–10,000 and serves multiple functions.

### Fining: Bentonite vs. Pea Protein

**Critical finding:** Research on Tămâioasă Românească (closely analogous aromatic profile to Muscat Ottonel) showed:
- Bentonite at standard doses reduced linalool by **~18.5%** and esters by **12–33%**
- Pea protein fining (without bentonite) **increased linalool by 16.7–19.3%** vs. control

**Protocol:**
- Conduct bench-trial fining tests on every vintage before scaling
- Use **minimum effective bentonite dose** (typically 0.5–1.5 g/L, not 2–4 g/L)
- Prioritise **pea protein fining** (Enartis Plantis, Laffort Oenofine Nature) — also a vegan certification claim
- A low-dose combination (pea protein + minimal bentonite) may be optimal for protein stability with minimal aroma loss

### SO₂ Management

| Stage | Dose | Form | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| At reception / pressing | 30–50 mg/kg grapes | KMBS solution | Inhibit oxidases, wild yeast, bacteria |
| Post-pressing, pre-ferment | 20–40 mg/L free SO₂ | KMBS | Protect juice during cold settling |
| Post-fermentation | 25–35 mg/L free SO₂ | KMBS | Microbial stability during storage |
| Pre-bottling | **20–30 mg/L free SO₂** | KMBS | EU max total SO₂: 200 mg/L |

At Huși's typical juice pH of 3.2–3.4, maintaining 20–25 mg/L free SO₂ at bottling yields 0.5–0.8 mg/L molecular SO₂ — the genuinely antimicrobial fraction. In-house measurement via Ripper titration (Hanna HI84500 mini-titrator or Vinmetrica SC-100A).

## 4.7 Oenological Inputs

### Yeast Selection

Muscat Ottonel carries ~37% of its terpene pool as free (immediately volatile) forms and the remainder as bound glycosides. The ideal yeast releases bound terpenes via **β-glucosidase activity** without producing off-notes.

| Strain | Supplier | β-glucosidase | Temp Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **LALVIN QA23** | Lallemand | High | 10–28°C | Widely available in Romania; ferments to dryness at 10°C; top recommendation |
| **Zymaflore VL1** | Laffort | High | 16–20°C | Specifically recommended for Muscat, Riesling, Gewürztraminer; low VA |
| **LALVIN ICV D47** | Lallemand | High | 15–20°C | High glycerol; citrus and floral notes; good for Muscat and Pinot Gris |
| SafOeno HD T18 | Fermentis | High | 14–20°C | Positioned for terpenic white wines |

**Protocol:** Start with QA23 as the primary strain; run a comparative 10–20 hl lot with VL1 in the first two vintages to establish house preference. Inoculation rate: 20–30 g/hL. Cost: €3–8/kg; for 50 tonnes of grapes: **€30–80 per vintage**.

### Enzymes

| Product | Supplier | Activity | Application |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lallzyme Cuvée Blanc | Lallemand | Pectinase | Must clarification + press aid; 1–3 g/hL |
| EnartisZym Arom MP | Enartis | Pectinase + glycosidase | Aromatic precursor release; 2–4 g/hL |
| Rapidase AR 2000 | Oenobrands/DSM | Glycosidase | Post-fermentation aroma release on lees |

Pectolytic enzymes: improve juice yield 5–10%, accelerate cold settling, and release bound terpenes from skin cell vacuoles. Add at 8–15°C during cold settling; contact time 4–12 hours.

### Nutrient Additions

Muscat Ottonel musts from Huși's clay-loam soils can be low in YAN (50–100 mg/L N). Target: **150–200 mg/L N** for clean aromatic fermentation.

1. **Rehydration:** GoFerm Protect Evolution or Nutriferm Energy at 30 g/hL in the yeast rehydration water
2. **Addition 1** (48–72 hours post-inoculation): Fermaid-O at 30–40 g/hL — organic nitrogen is metabolised 3–4× more efficiently than DAP and produces less fusel alcohol
3. **Addition 2** (if needed, at ⅓ sugar depletion): Fermaid-O (20 g/hL) + minimal DAP (20 g/hL)

**Avoid DAP** as the primary nitrogen source at low fermentation temperatures (12–14°C) — it promotes fusel alcohol production and can mask varietal aromatics. Many premium Muscat producers use zero DAP.

### Cold Maceration (Experimental)

Brief cold skin contact can increase free terpene levels in Muscat Ottonel but carries oxidation risk. If exploring:
- **Conditions:** 6–8°C, 2–4 hours maximum, under inert gas (CO₂ or N₂), with 30–50 mg/kg SO₂ added at crushing, with pectolytic enzymes
- **Approach:** Trial as a separate 20–30 hl lot in the first vintage; do not commit full production until results are evaluated
- **Risk:** Extended time, higher temperature, or inadequate SO₂ causes irreversible phenolic browning

### SO₂ Alternatives

- **Lysozyme** (250–500 mg/L): Prevents unwanted MLF in white wine by lysing lactic acid bacteria. Useful if LAB counts are elevated post-fermentation. Contains egg allergen — must be declared on label (or confirmed removed by bentonite fining).
- **DMDC / Velcorin** (≤200 mg/L): Broad-spectrum antimicrobial added just before bottling; critical for off-dry/demi-sec Muscat Ottonel (8–20 g/L residual sugar) where sterile filtration alone may not fully prevent refermentation. Requires dedicated dosing equipment; typically accessed via contract bottling.

## 4.9 AFIR Grant Opportunities

### IS-V-02 / IS-V-07 — National Wine Sector Support Programme (APIA)

- **Grant rate:** Up to **50% of eligible costs** (EU FEGA funding)
- **Maximum support:** €400,000 (simple investments) / €1,500,000 (complex)
- **Eligible costs:** Tanks, presses, chillers, filters, bottling lines, lab equipment, tasting room, ANSVSA compliance works, consulting, software
- **Eligibility:** ONVPV-registered producers; legal entity, authorised natural person, or family enterprise
- **Commitment:** 5-year maintenance of investment

### DR-23 — Agricultural Processing Investments (AFIR, CAP 2023–2027)

- **Grant rate:** Up to **65% of eligible costs**
- **Maximum:** Up to €3 million per project
- **Note:** Wine (NACE 1102) eligibility in specific call rounds must be confirmed with AFIR directly before designing the application

### Grant Strategy

1. Register with ONVPV as the immediate prerequisite for wine sector subsidies
2. Apply to IS-V-02 or IS-V-07 in Year 2–3 once the winery is operational and producing DOC wine
3. Monitor DR-23 calls; at 65% co-financing, major Phase 2 equipment purchases become very cost-effective
4. Used equipment sourcing (Vinimat, Arsilac, Agriaffaires) can reduce equipment capex by 40–60% vs. new — grant co-financing combined with used equipment purchasing is the highest-leverage financial strategy

## 4.10 Indigenous Variety Winemaking Notes

### Zghihară de Huși

The definitive regional white variety of Huși; Domeniile Averești grows 40 ha commercially. Winemaking follows the same basic protocol as Muscat Ottonel (pneumatic press, cool fermentation, stainless steel) but benefits from:
- Brief lees contact (sur lie, 2–3 months) for texture — Zghihară's high natural acidity supports this
- Can benefit from brief contact with neutral used oak (no new oak flavour transfer)
- Lower botrytis risk than Muscat Ottonel; picking timing is more flexible

### Busuioacă de Bohotin

An aromatic pink-skinned variety with rose petal and basil notes (loosely compared to a light Gewürztraminer). Processing options:
- **Direct-press rosé:** minimal skin contact during pressing; pale pink, fresh, off-dry
- **Short maceration rosé:** 2–6 hours skin contact before pressing; deeper pink, more aromatic
- Market standard: off-dry or demi-sec styles; Velcorin (DMDC) addition at bottling essential for microbiological stability if RS > 4 g/L

Both varieties are approved for DOC Dealurile Huși and strengthen the regional provenance narrative alongside the Muscat Ottonel flagship.
