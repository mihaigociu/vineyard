### Market Growth Trend

| Period | Volume trend | Value trend | Notes |
|--------|-------------|-------------|-------|
| 2019–2020 | Stable | Stable | HoReCa disrupted by pandemic; retail compensated |
| 2020–2021 | +~5% | +~10% | Strong rebound; market value peaks at RON 2.1bn |
| 2021–2022 | -8.4% | +6.5% | Drought + inflation; premiumisation offsets decline |
| 2022–2023 | +14% (calendar yr) | +modest | Strong harvest partially restored volumes |
| 2023–2024 | -11.4% | +10.1% avg bottle value | Consumer squeeze + health trends; premiumisation continues |
| 2018–2023 CAGR | +1.8% (modern trade) | — | [Just-Drinks/GlobalData](https://www.just-drinks.com/data-insights/still-wine-market-size-romania/) |

**Key insight:** Volume is structurally flat-to-declining. Value is growing at ~6–10% per year in the premium segment. A quality-focused bottled wine can grow revenue even in a contracting volume market.

### Per Capita Consumption

Romania: **~18.7 litres/person/year** (2024) vs EU average ~28.7 L — roughly 10L below average.
This indicates significant headroom for market growth if consumer habits shift.

Source: [Visual Capitalist 2025](https://www.visualcapitalist.com/ranked-the-countries-that-drink-the-most-wine-per-capita/) | [Revistaprogresiv.ro](https://revistaprogresiv.ro/analize/fmcg/piata-vinului-pe-o-traiectorie-anevoioasa-spre-maturizare-si-rafinare/)

*Note: Romanian statistics are complicated by large subsistence/homebrew production that never enters official figures. True market-sold per-capita consumption is lower.*

### Bulk vs Bottled Split

- **~70% of production** = wines without geographical designation (bulk/table) — [Economica.net/ONVPV](https://www.economica.net/piata-vinului-a-scazut-anul-trecut-cu-27-in-volum-consumatorii-se-reorienteaza-incet-catre-vinurile-de-calitate718593_718593.html)
- Bulk/table consumption fell **-33% YoY** (2022–23 viticultural year) — the steepest decline of any category
- **PDO wines** (DOC): 800,524 hl produced in 2022/23
- **PGI wines**: the *only category gaining share*, up **+21.8% volume** in 2022/23

**Implication:** Do not sell as table wine. Register for DOC (Dealurile Husi) or minimum PGI designation from the start.

### Domestic vs Imported Wine

- ~**90% of production** consumed domestically — strong home-market preference
- Imports: **€268.4m / 123,000 tonnes** (2023); exports: **€72.5m / 34,400 tonnes** — Romania imports 3.7× more by value than it exports
- Import origins: Moldova, Ukraine, Italy (dominant volume sources)
- Imports' share of domestic consumption: ~5% (up from ~3% five years ago)

Source: [Retail-fmcg.ro/Wines of Romania](https://www.retail-fmcg.ro/fmcg/wines-of-romaniaimport-vin.html) | [Romania-insider.com](https://www.romania-insider.com/romanians-prefer-drink-local-wine-2023)

### Consumer Trends

| Trend | Data point | Implication |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Indigenous varieties | Feteasca Neagra production nearly tripled 2019–2023 | Muscat Ottonel is well-known locally; can benefit from "native/authentic" positioning |
| Premiumisation | +10.1% avg bottle value YoY to May 2025 | Supports mid-premium pricing strategy |
| Organic/natural | Natural wine preferences +150% over 5 years | Organic certification = strong differentiator; no major Husi producer is certified organic |
| Dry over sweet | Shift from sweet/off-dry to dry among younger urban consumers | Dry Muscat Ottonel is an underserved style in the region |
| White wine growth | Whites = 54.7% of retail volume; reds declining -5% in 2024 | Muscat Ottonel is well-timed in the right colour |
| New winery growth | 473 authorised wineries by Jan 2024 (+12% in under a year) | Market is growing in diversity; craft producers gaining legitimacy |

Sources: [Wine Intelligence 2023](https://wine-intelligence.com/blogs/wine-news-insights-wine-intelligence-trends-data-reports/romanian-wine-boom-over-10-surge-in-local-vineyards-2023) | [Dor De Vin 2024](https://www.dordevin.ro/en/anul-2024-in-industria-vinului-din-romania-un-an-al-recordurilor-si-recunoasterii-internationale/) | [Revistaprogresiv.ro 2025](https://revistaprogresiv.ro/analize/fmcg/piata-vinului-pe-o-traiectorie-anevoioasa-spre-maturizare-si-rafinare/) | [Horeca.ro 2024](https://www.horeca.ro/food-beverage/tendinte-din-industria-vinului-in-anul-2024/)

|-----------|--------|

| Vineyard area | ~1,400–1,700 ha |

### Market Gaps & Opportunities Summary

| Gap | Evidence | Opportunity |
|-----|----------|-------------|
| Dedicated Muscat Ottonel from Husi | Casa de Vinuri Husi doesn't produce it; Averesti/Vincon do but not as flagship | Hero wine with clear regional identity |
| Dry/off-dry Muscat Ottonel style | Consumer trend toward dry/demi-sec; most Romanian Muscat is semi-sweet/sweet | Underserved style in the region |
| Organic/sustainable Husi wine | No major Husi producer is certified organic | Strong domestic + EU export differentiator |
| Boutique terroir-driven positioning | All major producers are industrial (100s of ha) | Artisan story + quality not available to volume players |
| Wine tourism / cellar door | Moldova wine tourism underdeveloped | Growing domestic demand; Husi is ~2.5h from Iași |
| Zghihară de Huși as co-variety | Only ~85–90 ha nationally; growing market interest | Hyper-local variety with strong export narrative |

## 2.3 EU Export Potential

### Romanian Wine Export Volumes and Value

| Year | Volume (tonnes) | Value (EUR million) | Avg price (EUR/litre) |
|------|----------------|--------------------|-----------------------|
| 2021 | ~38,000 | ~69 | 1.82 |
| 2022 | ~36,300 | ~71.6 | 1.97 |
| 2023 | ~34,400 | ~72.5 | 2.09 |
| 2024 | ~16–20,000 est. | ~USD 42m | ~2.2 (USD/litre) |

Volume is on a slight declining trend; value growing (+5% over 2021–2023). Export value **nearly doubled** 2014–2024 (USD 22m → USD 42m) while volumes held roughly flat — classic premiumisation pattern.
Romania exports only **~5–6% of total production** — domestic market absorbs 90%+.
Source: [Retail-fmcg.ro/Wines of Romania](https://www.retail-fmcg.ro/fmcg/wines-of-romaniaimport-vin.html) | [IndexBox Romania 2025](https://www.indexbox.io/store/romania-wine-market-report-analysis-and-forecast-to-2020/)

### Romanian Wine Styles Gaining Traction in EU

- **Fetească Neagră** (red): Leading export ambassador; production tripled 2019–2023; strong award performance
- **Fetească Regală / Albă** (white): Rising — described as Romania's next "visit card" for white wine
- **Tămâioasă Românească** (aromatic white): Among most appreciated for export
- **Busuioacă de Bohotin** (pink aromatic): Gaining recognition; Averești leading exporter

Leading export wineries: **Cramele Recaș** (largest, ~€72m revenue, targeting €100m), **Domeniile Averești**, **Budureasca**.
Source: [The Drinks Business — Cramele Recas](https://www.thedrinksbusiness.com/2024/09/cramele-recas-invests-e11-million-to-reach-e100m-turnover/) | [Sommelier Choice Awards](https://sommelierschoiceawards.com/en/blog/interviews-3373/romanian-wines-a-rising-star-on-the-global-stage-953.htm)

### Muscat Ottonel & Aromatic Whites in EU Markets

- Global Muscat wine market: **USD 2.14 billion (2024)**, projected **USD 3.23 billion by 2033** (CAGR 4.7%); Europe ~47% of sales — [DataIntelo](https://dataintelo.com/report/muscat-wine-market)
- Romanian Muscat Ottonel already in UK retail: Jidvei Traditional Muscat Ottonel — [Romanian Drinks UK](https://romaniandrinks.co.uk/products/jidvei-traditional-muscat-ottonel-white-romanian-wine)
- Natural/minimal-intervention Muscat Ottonel emerging in EU natural wine shops — [MORE Natural Wine](https://morenaturalwine.com/collections/muskat-ottonel)
- Muscat Ottonel is **not yet among Romania's leading export varieties** (those are Feteasca Neagra, Tamaioasa, Feteasca Regala) — but benefits from growing aromatic wine trend
- Dry/off-dry Muscat Ottonel from an organic producer could find niche in EU natural wine and aromatic white markets

## Key Takeaways for Husi Vineyard Decision

| Finding | Strategic implication |
|---------|-----------------------|
| Market value growing +10.1%/yr despite volume decline | Quality over quantity — premium positioning is the right bet |
| Bulk/table wine collapsing -33%; PGI/PDO growing | Pursue DOC Dealurile Husi designation from Year 1 |
| White wine = 54.7% of retail volume, growing | Muscat Ottonel is in the right category at the right time |
| No major Husi producer makes Muscat Ottonel a hero wine | Clearest product gap in the immediate geography |
| Natural wine demand +150% in 5 years; no organic Husi producer | Organic conversion should be on the roadmap (3-year path) |
| Carrefour "Deschidem Vinul Românesc" programme | Concrete route-to-shelf for a small new producer |
| Export = only 5–6% of Romanian production but value is growing | Domestic market first; EU export a Phase 3 objective |
| UK export surged +506% in 2024 | UK is the highest-growth EU-adjacent market for Romanian wine |
